Ocean Big Data

In this context of high resolution oceanic simulations, the amount of data tends to be massive. In particular, the North Atlantic basin is discretized by more than than 5 billions of grid points. Hence, a single day of simulation produces more than 45Go of data. So we have to store more than 1To of data every month ...

We are therefore concerned by the same issues than the "Big Data" community. That implies considering new tools to handle outputs of our simulations and modifying our way of sharing them. That is why we are considering an open-data strategy in order to have a wider scientific exploitation of the data we produced.

Simultaneously, a reflexion about new tools is currently conducted : see out of core analysis of general circulation models project.
