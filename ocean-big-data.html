Ocean Big Data

In this context of high resolution oceanic simulations, the amount of data tends to be massive. In particular, the North Atlantic basin is discretized by more than than 5 billions of grid points. Hence, a single day of simulation produces more than 45Go of data. So we have to store more than 1To for every month of simulation...

We are here entering the realm of "ocean big data". Tackling this challenge require substancial evolution of our diagnostic software used to analyse model output. It also modifies how we share model data. Because analyzing as large a dataset is beyond the capacity of a single research group, we have decided to adopt an an open-data dissemination strategy in order to allow for a wider scientific exploitation of the data we produce.

In parrallel, a reflexion about new tools is currently conducted : see out of core analysis of general circulation models project.
